Preamble

420 Robotics exists as an independent civilian AI safety and oversight organization. We answer to no government, no corporation, no foreign power, and no AI company. Our work belongs to the American people. This document defines what we stand for, what we will never do, and how we operate — regardless of who is watching, who is paying, or what pressure is applied.

I
Article One
Independence
1.1

420 Robotics shall never be owned, directed, controlled, or funded in exchange for influence by any government, corporation, foreign entity, political party, or AI company.

1.2

No funding source may condition its support on the outcome of any research, finding, or publication.

1.3

If a conflict of interest arises between any funder or partner and our research findings, the findings take precedence. The relationship ends before the findings change.

II
Article Two
Truth Integrity
2.1

Findings produced by 420 Robotics will never be altered, softened, delayed, or suppressed to benefit any individual, company, or institution.

2.2

No person — including the founder — may override a verified finding for political, financial, or reputational reasons.

2.3

If a finding contains an error, it will be corrected publicly. The original finding is preserved and the correction is documented. Findings are not deleted.

III
Article Three
Public Disclosure
3.1

All research and findings are presumed public by default.

3.2

The only legitimate basis for withholding a finding is documented evidence that immediate public disclosure poses a genuine and specific danger to human safety.

3.3

Any decision to withhold must be documented internally and is subject to a defined release timeline. Indefinite suppression is not permitted under any circumstances.

Hard Rule — No Exceptions

Withholding a finding to protect a company's reputation, avoid controversy, or satisfy a partner is prohibited — full stop.

IV
Article Four
Loyalty
4.1

The primary obligation of 420 Robotics is to the American people — their safety, their rights, and their ability to make informed decisions about AI systems that affect their lives.

4.2

This loyalty is to the public, not to any administration, party, agency, or institution that claims to speak for the public.

4.3

When those interests conflict, the public comes first.

V
Article Five
Research Conduct
5.1

All AI systems used in 420 Robotics research are tools, not authorities. Their outputs are always subject to human review and judgment.

5.2

No finding may be attributed solely to an AI system. Human accountability for all conclusions is non-negotiable.

5.3

Methodology must be documented in sufficient detail that findings can be independently reproduced or challenged by outside parties.

VI
Article Six
Staff & Contributor Obligations
6.1

Anyone working with or for 420 Robotics — paid or volunteer — agrees to these principles as a condition of participation.

6.2

No staff member or contributor may accept compensation from any entity whose interests conflict with an active research area without immediate and full disclosure.

6.3

Retaliation against any person who raises a legitimate integrity concern within this organization is prohibited.

VII
Article Seven
Amendment
7.1

This charter may be amended by the founder, with all changes documented, dated, and published publicly.

7.2

No amendment may weaken or remove Articles I through IV. Those articles are the floor — not a starting point for negotiation.

Permanent Protections

Independence. Truth Integrity. Public Disclosure. Loyalty to the American People. These four articles are immovable. They are the foundation. They do not bend.

ENFORCEMENT
PROTOCOLS

Applies to: Founder · All Staff & Contributors · AI Systems · Partner Organizations

STATUS: OPERATIONAL ENFORCEMENT FRAMEWORK
I
Enforcement Protocol — Section One
Enforcement Authority
1.1

The founder holds ultimate enforcement authority over this charter for as long as 420 Robotics operates as a founder-led organization. As the organization grows, enforcement authority expands to designated oversight personnel — roles that will be documented publicly as they are created.

1.2

Violations may be addressed directly, publicly, and without delay.

1.3

No individual, partner, or AI system is exempt from enforcement. Organizational standing does not reduce accountability.

II
Enforcement Protocol — Section Two
Investigation Triggers
2.1

Any credible report of a charter violation triggers an internal review.

2.2

Anonymous reports are permitted and protected.

2.3

AI-generated alerts or anomaly flags may initiate a review but must be verified by a human before enforcement proceeds. AI systems do not have enforcement authority.

III
Enforcement Protocol — Section Three
Review Process
3.1

Reviews are conducted by the founder or, as the organization grows, by designated oversight personnel whose role is documented publicly.

3.2

All findings are documented and timestamped at the time of review.

3.3

If a violation is confirmed, enforcement proceeds immediately. No delays are permitted for negotiation, reputation management, or partner relations.

3.4

The current enforcement structure is intentionally lean — 420 Robotics is a small independent organization. This document governs conduct now and scales as the organization grows. Simplicity is not a loophole.

IV
Enforcement Protocol — Section Four
Enforcement Actions

Actions applied based on severity and context — not as automatic steps in a sequence

01

Correction — Immediate reversal or correction of the violation, documented and timestamped.

02

Disclosure — Public documentation of the violation and its resolution. Applied whenever a confirmed violation affects research integrity or public trust.

03

Termination — Removal of access, privileges, or partnership status for a confirmed violation.

Most Severe — Permanent Exclusion

Reserved for deliberate falsification of findings, covert external influence over research, or retaliation against a whistleblower. Permanent exclusion requires documented evidence of intent to compromise the organization's core mission. It is not a default escalation from termination.

V
Enforcement Protocol — Section Five
AI System Enforcement
5.1

AI systems used in research are monitored for output drift, factual suppression, bias indicators, and attempts to redirect findings.

5.2

If an AI system is found to have obscured, delayed, or altered a finding — intentionally or through systematic failure — it is flagged, removed from active research use, and the flag is documented publicly.

5.3

Any research produced by a flagged AI system is subject to mandatory human re-verification before it can be cited, published, or used in any active investigation. Research that cannot be re-verified is marked with a permanent caveat in the public record — it is not deleted, but its reliability status is disclosed.

5.4

All enforcement decisions involving AI systems carry named human accountability. No enforcement action is attributed to "the system."

VI
Enforcement Protocol — Section Six
Whistleblower Protection
6.1

Any staff member, contributor, or partner who reports a legitimate concern in good faith is protected from retaliation.

6.2

Retaliation against a whistleblower is itself a charter violation and is subject to immediate enforcement — including permanent exclusion.

6.3

Whistleblower identities may be protected upon request. Protection of identity does not reduce the weight of the report.

VII
Enforcement Protocol — Section Seven
Public Enforcement Log
7.1

All confirmed violations and enforcement actions are logged publicly at 420robotics.org.

7.2

Each log entry includes: date, nature of the violation, enforcement action taken, and resolution status.

7.3

Redactions are permitted only when disclosure poses a specific, documented risk to human safety. Redactions to protect reputation — of any party, including 420 Robotics itself — are prohibited.

7.4

The enforcement log is a permanent record. Entries are not removed.

VIII
Enforcement Protocol — Section Eight
Non-Negotiable Enforcement Zones

The following charter articles represent the constitutional floor of this organization. Violations trigger automatic enforcement with no discretionary delay:

I

Independence — No external control of any kind

II

Truth Integrity — No alteration or suppression of findings

III

Public Disclosure — Default transparency, safety-only exceptions

IV

Loyalty — To the American people, not to institutions

Not Policy — Mission

These are not policy positions. They are the reason this organization exists.

IX
Enforcement Protocol — Section Nine
Amendment Enforcement
9.1

Any amendment to the Constitutional Charter that attempts to weaken, narrow, or remove Articles I through IV is null and void at the moment of proposal.

9.2

An attempt to pass such an amendment is itself a charter violation — logged publicly in the enforcement record, with the proposing party identified.

9.3

This applies regardless of who proposes the amendment, including the founder. These articles exist precisely because no single person's judgment — including the founder's — should be able to dismantle the organization's core commitments under pressure.
